Heads up: this simulation uses flicker and occasional light flashes. If you're sensitive to flashing light, please take care. It's an educational approximation, not a medical tool.

Visual Snow Syndrome

VSS is a neurological condition affecting an estimated 2–3% of people. Its hallmark is visual snow: dynamic, continuous tiny dots across the entire visual field — like watching the world through television static or a shaken snow globe, 24/7, with eyes open or closed.

Per the published diagnostic criteria, a diagnosis also requires at least two of these four symptom categories, all of which this simulator can render:

  • Palinopsia — afterimages and trailing that linger after the object is gone.
  • Enhanced entoptic phenomena — excessive floaters, swarms of darting dots (blue-field phenomenon), and spontaneous flashes of light (photopsia).
  • Photophobia — sensitivity or intolerance to light.
  • Nyctalopia — impaired night vision.

Many people also experience halos and starbursts around lights, double vision, and pulsating or trembling vision — plus non-visual symptoms like tinnitus, brain fog, and sensory overload that no screen can show.

This simulator is an approximation for empathy and education — everyone's VSS is different, and it is not a diagnostic or medical tool. Learn more at the Visual Snow Initiative.
